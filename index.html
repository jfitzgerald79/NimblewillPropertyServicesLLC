Geographic Information Systems (GIS)

A GIS is more than making maps! A GIS allows users to visualize data, to interpret data, and to understand data through relationships and patterns. A GIS provides organizations with a method to make decisive decisions for real estate site selection, construction site selection, project planning, conservation, environmental site analysis, and data analysis. A GIS benefits customers from all industries allowing to manage their property with spatial and data organization, provide customers with visualization of progress, interoperability between software, and add a unique marketing strategy to their company.

GIS Production

There are many ways a GIS can be represented. A GIS can be shared through reports, maps, tables, and charts. It can be delivered in printed format or digital format. It really depends on the use of a GIS. If the client wants a general purpose map of their property, then a printed format or digital format of their property is appropriate. If the client wants to be able to use GIS on a daily basis for the purpose of viewing data, then perhaps creating an internet web map is appropriate. If an organization wants to use GIS software to create data, store data, and manage data, then options can be discussed to migrate an organization to the appropriate GIS software.
